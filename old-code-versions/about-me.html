DOWNLOAD CV

Described by BAFTA winning Patrick Titley as ‘cool.’

I first got into film when I joined a club at my local youth centre. Within months of joining the club I attended a workshop at the SON&R centre at BBC Bristol, due to being a Young Correspondents runner up and I also gained a place with TVYP at the Bristol Endemol Studios.

From here, I went on to win 3rd Place in a local film festival and knew this was the direction for me. During my University years, I found myself being widely involved in music related shows. I created The Sound Lab for YSTV and was part of the winning team that won Best Broadcaster at the National Student Television Awards 2012.

In 2012 I worked the London Paralympic Opening and Closing Ceremonies for Done & Dusted, and joined them again in November for the MOBO awards. My favourite part about these shows was getting glimpses into the directing skillset of Hamish Hamilton (probably still my favourite director to this day).

After being a runner for several months I joined Roll to Record at Elstree Studios and did my training to become a grade 5 operator for Big Brother, whilst at the same time freelancing in the ITV Breakfast Green Room.

I then moved across to ITV completely to become a Studio Technician and have stayed with them throughout several changes, where I am now a Senior Studio Technician helping to oversee a team of multiskilled assistants.

In the future, I wish to become a director of live television events, for example extreme sporting competitions or award ceremonies (with musical content being an added bonus!) and keep my involvement in skateboarding culture through new and exciting projects.
